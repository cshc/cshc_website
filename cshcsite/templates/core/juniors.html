Juniors

Hockey training for boys and girls aged 6 - 12
on Saturday mornings

For the 2016/17 hockey season, Cambridge South Hockey Club is establishing a new junior section. Training sessions will run weekly on Saturday mornings during the hockey season led by a professional coach assisted by club player-coaches.

The sessions are for boys and girls, aimed at ages 6-12 (please contact us for ages outside this range). Training is based around the ‘Quicksticks’ form of the game played in schools, using larger, lighter and safer balls. The junior sessions are focused on teaching and developing basic skills and team play, ultimately providing a pathway towards senior team hockey (minimum age 13).

Full season or ‘taster programme’ subscriptions are available. The ‘taster’ subscription covers 3 consecutive Saturdays, for new players wanting to try out hockey before committing to a full membership. Upgrade simply by paying the balance!

No previous hockey experience is necessary and beginners are welcomed. All sticks, balls and equipment are provided, but players may bring their own stick if they have one (please label clearly in permanent marker). Players require comfortable sportswear and trainers with grippy soles.

To apply for a place, or for more information, please fill in the contact form on this page. Following confirmation of a place, a Registration and Consent form must be completed and returned, together with payment, before a player may attend a session.

Contacts

Juniors Secretary - Neil Sneade

As well as turning out for the Men’s 3rd XI, Neil acts as Club Secretary and now also Junior Secretary. He has two daughters aged 8 and 6 and will be assisting with junior coaching.

Welfare Officer - Jack Chalk

Responsible for the club’s welfare policy and procedures, Jack is a teacher by profession with extensive experience in child welfare and protection. He is the main point of contact for any issues or concerns relating to junior player welfare.

Welfare Advisor - Ania Rygielska

Since joining the club in the Ladies 3rd XI development side, Ania has qualified as an umpire and taken on responsibility for arranging the club’s umpiring. She has previous experience working with children in volunteer roles.

Juniors Coach - Colin Hulett

An experienced PE teacher and qualified hockey coach, he has coached children of various ages in numerous sports and also acts as one of the club’s senior team coaches.
